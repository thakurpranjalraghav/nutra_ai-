Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of medicine, recognizes three fundamental energies or doshas that govern all physiological and psychological processes in the body: Vata (air and space), Pitta (fire and water), and Kapha (earth and water).

Your constitution, or Prakriti, is determined at conception and remains constant throughout your life. However, your current state, or Vikriti, can fluctuate based on lifestyle, diet, stress, and environmental factors.

Understanding your constitution helps you make informed choices about diet, exercise, and lifestyle practices that support your natural balance and promote optimal health.

Ayurveda has been practiced for over 5,000 years, making it one of the world's oldest healing systems. The foundational texts, including the Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita, were written between 400-200 BCE.

The concept of constitutional types was developed through centuries of observation and clinical practice by ancient physicians who recognized that individuals respond differently to foods, treatments, and environmental conditions.

This personalized approach to health and nutrition predates modern precision medicine by millennia, demonstrating the timeless wisdom of individualized healthcare.

Summer (Pitta Season)

During hot months, Pitta types should be especially mindful of staying cool and avoiding excessive heat. Focus on cooling foods and activities.

Winter (Kapha Season)

Cold, damp weather can increase Kapha, so Pitta types may benefit from warming spices and more vigorous exercise during this time.

Fall/Spring (Vata Season)

Transitional seasons can increase Vata, so maintain regular routines and eat grounding, nourishing foods.

Climate Adaptation

Your location's climate will influence which seasonal recommendations are most important for maintaining balance.
